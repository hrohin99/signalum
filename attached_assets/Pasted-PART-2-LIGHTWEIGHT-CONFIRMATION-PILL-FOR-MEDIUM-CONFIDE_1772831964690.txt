PART 3 — ASPECT SELECTION MODAL
This modal appears in two situations:
- User clicks "No, change this" on the confirmation banner
- Sibling inference returns low confidence AND Claude enrichment finds no ambiguity (single company identified but no domain context to infer from)
The modal:
- Title: "What do you want to track about [entityName]?"
- Subtext: "[entityName] operates across multiple areas. Tell us what matters to you so we only surface relevant intelligence."
- Display 3-6 aspect pills generated by Claude from the company's known business units. Each pill is a rounded button with the business unit name. Example for Entrust: "Identity Verification", "Digital Certificates and PKI", "Card Issuance", "All business areas"
- A free text option at the bottom: "Something else —" with a text input
- No cancel button — user must make a selection to proceed
Generate the aspect pills dynamically using Claude:
"List the 3-5 main business units or product areas of [entityName] ([company_website]). Return as a simple array of short labels, maximum 4 words each."
When user selects an aspect:
- Save selection as disambiguation_context
- Set disambiguation_confirmed true
- Close modal
- Trigger Perplexity search scoped to selected aspect
- Show green toast: "Watchloom will now track [entityName] for [selected aspect]."
